## The Leaky Funnel

Most home service businesses generate plenty of interest. Then it leaks. Old leads sit untouched, inquiries wait too long, missed calls never get recovered, and reviews get requested whenever someone remembers.

That is not a marketing strategy. It is an expensive way to lose revenue you already paid to create.

## The Numbers Don't Lie

A typical home service funnel over 30 days looks like this:

- **248 leads captured** — from ads, website forms, phone calls, walk-ins
- **71 replied to in time** — just 29% get a human response
- **23 actually booked** — 9% conversion from lead to appointment
- **177 leads went cold** — waiting for a reply that never came

**Sources:** [Lead Response Management Study](https://www.leadresponsemanagement.org/lrm_study/) · [HBR: The Short Life of Online Sales Leads](https://hbr.org/2011/03/the-short-life-of-online-sales-leads)

## Why It Happens

The average business takes **42 hours** to respond to a lead. By then, the homeowner has already bought from someone else. Meanwhile, their CRM database is full of contacts they paid to acquire — and never contacted again.

The result: more booked appointments, more reviews, and more revenue from leads you already paid for.
